The Three Pillars of Blockchain: Decentralization, Immutability, and Transparency

Blockchain technology is built on three fundamental pillars that make it so revolutionary. Understanding these is key to understanding blockchain itself.

1. Decentralization

Instead of information being held in one central place, it's spread across a huge network of computers. This means no single person or entity has control. This is the most critical pillar.

2. Immutability

This means something is unchanging over time. Once a transaction is recorded on the blockchain, it cannot be altered or deleted. This is not like a normal file1 you can just edit. 

        Centralized:  A -> B <- C
        Decentralized: A <-> B <-> C <-> A
3. Transparency

For public blockchains, all transactions are visible to anyone who has access to the network. This creates a level of trust and accountability. It's not a secret system. Only admins can see it. It's hidden from users. It's open for public verification.

Pillars Summary
Pillar Benefit
Decentralization No single point of failure.
Immutability Tamper-proof record keeping.
Transparency Builds trust among participants.

Together, these three pillars create a powerful new way to manage data.
