The Three Pillars of Blockchain

Blockchain technology stands on three core principles that make it powerful and trustworthy: Decentralization, Immutability, and Transparency. They work together to create a system that's secure, fair, and reliable.

Decentralization

 This means there's no central authority controlling the network. Instead, blockchain spreads control across many computers (nodes) worldwide. Everyone in the network has a copy of the data, and decisions are made by group agreement. This reduces risks like hacking or corruption. A single person can shut it down. One company owns it. This is truly distributed power.

Immutability

 Immutability ensures that once data is added to the blockchain, it can't be changed or deleted. It's like writing in permanent ink1. Each block uses cryptographic hashes to link to the previous block. Altering one would break the chain, alerting the network. This builds trust because records are tamper-proof.

Transparency
 Transparency means all transactions are visible to everyone on the network, but personal details stay private through encryption. The ledger is public, so anyone can check transactions, but identities are hidden behind codes.
  • Fosters accountability and trust.
  • Balances openness with privacy.

  3. Transparency keeps it honest.
