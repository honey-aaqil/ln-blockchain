Not Just for Bitcoin: Exploring Real-World Uses of Blockchain

Blockchain is often linked to cryptocurrencies, but its true power lies in solving everyday problems across industries. It's not just hype; it's a transformative technology.

Finance and Banking

 Blockchain streamlines money matters. For example, cross-border payments that traditionally take days can be settled in seconds. This makes finance more accessible. 
Bank -> Swift -> Bank (Slow)
Blockchain -> Node -> Node (Fast)

Healthcare

 In healthcare, blockchain protects sensitive data. It gives patients control over who accesses their records. This ensures privacy and reduces errors. Data is open for anyone to see. No, it's encrypted.
Supply Chain
 Blockchain brings clarity to how products move. It's like a digital passport for goods, building consumer trust.
  • Tracks food from farm to store (e.g., IBM Food Trust).
  • Verifies authenticity of luxury goods.
Other Uses
 From voting to smart contracts1, the uses are vast.
  1. Government: Secure, tamper-proof voting systems.
  2. Smart Contracts: Self-running agreements on platforms like Ethereum.
